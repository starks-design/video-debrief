Summary

Key Insights

  • The keynote's thesis: the inference inflection has arrived — compute demand is up 1,000,000× in two years, and "tokens are the new commodity."
  • 50× performance-per-watt on Grace Blackwell NVLink 72 vs Hopper — Jensen claimed 35×, then SemiAnalysis' Dylan Patel measured 50× ("accused me of sandbagging").
  • Vera Rubin unveiled as NVIDIA's next platform — a fully vertically-integrated system, not a single chip ("that's just adorable").
  • The data center reframed as a token factory: performance-per-watt drives token cost, and NVIDIA claims the lowest cost per token in the world.
  • NemoClaw / OpenClaw agentic reference design + the NVIDIA Agent Toolkit — "every software company needs an agent strategy."
  • "The ChatGPT moment of self-driving cars has arrived" — physical AI via Alpamayo CoT, GR00T in Isaac Lab, and the Newton physics solver on NVIDIA Warp (built with Disney & DeepMind).

Topic

Highlights of Jensen Huang's GTC 2026 keynote, centered on the shift from training to inference at scale. NVIDIA frames the data center as a token factory and positions Vera Rubin, agentic AI (NemoClaw) and physical AI/robotics as the full-stack answer — under the pitch "vertically integrated but horizontally open."
